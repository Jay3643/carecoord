1. Getting Started & First Login

Seniority Connect is the care coordination platform for Seniority Healthcare. As an Admin, you have the highest level of access: all coordinator and supervisor capabilities, plus user management, region management, and system configuration.

Setting Up Your Account

  1. Check your email for an invitation from Seniority Connect with a setup link.
  2. Click the Setup Account link to open the setup wizard.
  3. Create a password (minimum 8 characters).
  4. Set up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) by scanning the QR code with your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy).
  5. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator to confirm.
  6. You are now ready to log in.

Logging In

  1. Navigate to the Seniority Connect login page.
  2. Enter your email address and password.
  3. Enter the 6-digit 2FA code from your authenticator app.
  4. If pending tickets exist for you, the Pending Import modal appears (see Section 2).
Tip: As the first Admin, you may need to set up your own account through a direct database configuration or initial deployment process. Subsequent admins can be invited through the Admin Panel.

2. Importing Pending Tickets on Login

On login, if unassigned tickets were originally sent to your email, a Pending Import modal appears.

  • All pending tickets are listed with checkboxes (checked by default), ticket IDs, subjects, and senders
  • Select All / Select None links and an "X of Y selected" counter

Your Options

  1. Import all: Click Import Selected with all boxes checked.
  2. Import some: Uncheck tickets you don't want, then click Import Selected.
  3. Import none: Click Leave All in Queue.

3. Understanding the Region Queue

The Region Queue shows all tickets across your assigned regions, grouped by assignee. Unassigned tickets appear first with "Pending To: [Name]" sub-folders showing intended recipients.

Key Features

  • Groups collapse/expand by clicking headers. Unassigned is always first.
  • Multi-recipient tickets show a purple "Sent to N" badge
  • Ticket IDs are region-based: CPA-0001, SNJ-0042, DV-0003
  • Filters: Status tabs (Active, Unassigned, Open, Waiting, Closed), Region dropdown, Tag filter, Search
  • My Queue: Shows only your personally assigned tickets

4. Opening & Working Tickets

Click any ticket to open it. You see the header (ID, status, subject, clock), message thread, action tabs (Reply, Internal Note, Chat, AI), and right sidebar (assignee, region, tags, audit details, time log).

Tabs & Multi-Tasking

  • Multiple tickets open as tabs. Click between them to switch.
  • Click X to close a tab (stops clock); click + to return to queue
  • Tabs persist across navigation to Email, Chat, Dashboard, etc.

Time Tracking

  • Clock auto-starts on open, continues from previous total
  • Stop manually with the Stop button; Start Clock to resume
  • One clock at a time; closing a tab auto-stops
  • Time Log shows total, per-user breakdown, and expandable sessions

Closing a Ticket

  1. Click Close, select a closure reason, optionally add a comment.
  2. Active chats are archived as notes. Ticket moves to Archive.

5. Composing Replies

  1. Open a ticket, click the Reply tab.
  2. Type your message. Optionally use AI Draft for AI-generated text.
  3. Attach files (max 10 MB each) via the paperclip icon.
  4. Click Send (single recipient) or Reply All (all original recipients).
Tip: Replying to an unassigned ticket automatically assigns it to you.
Warning: Use Reply All carefully. Verify your message is appropriate for all recipients.

6. Internal Notes

  1. Open a ticket, click the Internal Note tab.
  2. Type your note and click Add Note.

Notes are team-only, permanent, never sent externally, and appear with a yellow background in the timeline. They persist after ticket closure.

7. Starting a Chat on a Ticket

  1. Open a ticket. Chat tab shows "Start a Chat" (gray) or "Open Chat" (blue).
  2. Click Start a Chat, select team members (at least one), click Start Discussion.
  3. Type messages and press Enter to send. Anyone who opens the chat tab is auto-joined.

Chats are archived as notes when the ticket is closed. Use "+ Add Team Members" to invite more people.

8. Creating New Messages

  1. Click New Message in the sidebar.
  2. Fill in To (autocomplete from contacts), Subject, Region, optional Tags, and message body.
  3. Use AI Draft or attach files (max 10 MB each).
  4. Click Send. A new ticket is created and assigned to you.

Minimize the compose modal with — to save your draft. Click the draft bar at the bottom-right to resume.

9. Personal Email Inbox

Click Email in the sidebar for full Gmail access. Browse folders and labels, search mail, read/reply/archive/delete/label/star messages.

Push to Queue Admin

Push individual or bulk-selected emails from your inbox into the ticket queue using the Push to Queue button. Emails become unassigned tickets in the appropriate region.

11. Archive

Click Archive to view closed tickets sorted by close date (25 per page). Search by subject, sender, or ticket ID. Click any ticket to see full history including messages, notes, and archived chats. As an admin, you can Reopen tickets (see Section 19).

12. Setting Active/Inactive Status

The Active/Inactive toggle is a Coordinator-only feature. As an Admin, your status is always Active. You can monitor coordinator statuses via the Activity Dashboard.

15. Activity Dashboard & User Audit Supervisor+

Click Activity in the sidebar. Five tabs:

1. Who's Online

Real-time user status (polls every 15 seconds). Click a user to drill down into their recent activity with time presets (15 min, 30 min, 1 hour).

2. Overview

Trend charts for ticket volume over 7/14/30 days: daily created, closed, and net open.

3. Team

Per-coordinator cards: open count, closed count, average response time, comparison bar charts.

4. Activity Feed

Filterable feed of all actions. Filter by user, action type, and time window.

5. User Audit

  1. Select a user and date range.
  2. Click Run Audit to see: summary cards (open, unread, closed, actions, avg response time), response time details per ticket, open tickets with read/unread status, action breakdown chart, and recent actions with clickable ticket links.
Tip: Use User Audit regularly to identify overloaded coordinators and redistribute work before response times suffer.

16. Audit Log Supervisor+

Click Audit Log in the sidebar. Each entry shows timestamp, user, action type, clickable ticket ID, and details. Filter by action type using the dropdown. All ticket IDs are clickable links that open the ticket directly.

Tip: Use the Audit Log to investigate issues. Click ticket links to jump directly to any ticket referenced in the log.

17. Pull from Queue Supervisor+

Pull removes tickets from Seniority Connect and returns the email to Gmail.

Single Ticket

  1. Open the ticket, click Pull from Queue.
  2. Choose: Return to original recipient (email goes to the coordinator's Gmail as unread) or Pull to my inbox (email goes to your Gmail as unread).
  3. Confirm. The ticket is removed from Seniority Connect.

Multiple Tickets

  1. Select tickets with checkboxes in the queue.
  2. Click Pull from Queue, choose destination, confirm.
  3. Toast confirms: "N pulled -- returned to original" or "sent to your inbox."
Warning: Pulling is irreversible within Seniority Connect. The ticket and its notes/chat are removed. The email must be re-imported through normal sync if needed later.

18. Bulk Reassign from Queue Supervisor+

  1. In the Region Queue, select tickets with checkboxes.
  2. Click Reassign To... in the bulk action bar.
  3. Select a team member from the dropdown, or choose "Unassigned" to return to queue.
  4. Reassigned tickets appear as unread/new for the recipient. An audit entry is recorded.
Tip: Check the Activity Dashboard's Team tab first to see who has capacity before reassigning.

19. Reopening Closed Tickets from Archive Supervisor+

  1. Go to Archive, find the ticket (browse or search).
  2. Click the ticket to open its detail view.
  3. Click the Reopen button (visible to supervisors and admins only).
  4. The ticket returns to the active queue as Open, with full history preserved.
Admin-Only Features The following sections cover system administration tools available exclusively to the Admin role.

20. Admin Panel — Managing Users Admin Only

Click Admin in the left sidebar, then select the Users tab. This is where you manage all user accounts in the system.

Viewing Users

The Users tab displays a table of all active users showing:

  • Name and email
  • Role (Coordinator, Supervisor, or Admin) shown as a colored badge
  • Assigned regions
  • Google Workspace connection status (connected/disconnected)
  • Action buttons: Edit, Deactivate, Reset Password, Manage Regions, Connect/Disconnect Workspace

Toggle "Show Inactive" to also display deactivated users (grayed out with a Reactivate option).

Inviting a New User

  1. Click the Invitations tab in the Admin Panel.
  2. Fill in the Full Name field.
  3. Enter the Email address. It must end with @seniorityhealthcare.com.
  4. Select a Role from the dropdown: Coordinator, Supervisor, or Admin.
  5. Check one or more Regions to assign the user to. At least one region is required.
  6. Click Send Invitation. The system sends an email with a setup link to the new user.
Tip: The invitation email contains a time-limited setup link. If it expires before the user completes setup, you can resend it from the Invitations table.

Managing Invitations

Below the invitation form, a table shows all sent invitations with status:

  • Pending (yellow) — Sent but not yet accepted
  • Accepted (green) — User has completed setup
  • Expired (red) — Link has expired without being used

For pending or expired invitations, you can:

  • Resend — Sends a fresh invitation email with a new setup link
  • Revoke — Cancels the invitation entirely

Editing a User

  1. Find the user in the Users table and click Edit.
  2. A modal opens with the user's current name, email, and role.
  3. Modify any fields as needed:
    • Name — Update the display name
    • Email — Change their email address
    • Role — Promote or change role (Coordinator, Supervisor, Admin)
  4. Update their Region assignments by checking/unchecking regions.
  5. Click Save. Changes take effect immediately.

Deactivating a User

  1. Find the user in the Users table and click Deactivate.
  2. A confirmation prompt appears: "Deactivate [Name]? Their open tickets will be returned to the queue."
  3. Click OK to confirm.

When a user is deactivated:

  • They can no longer log in
  • All their open tickets are automatically returned to the unassigned region queue
  • Their email sync stops
  • They appear in the "Inactive" users list (visible when "Show Inactive" is toggled on)

Reactivating a User

  1. Toggle "Show Inactive" in the Users tab to reveal deactivated users.
  2. Find the user and click Reactivate.
  3. The user can log in again and will need to reclaim tickets from the queue.

Resetting a User's Password

  1. Find the user in the Users table and click Reset Password.
  2. The system generates a new temporary password and displays it on screen.
  3. Copy the temporary password and communicate it securely to the user (do not email it in plain text).
  4. The user will use this temporary password to log in and should change it immediately.
Important: The temporary password is shown only once. Make sure to copy it before closing the dialog. If you lose it, you can reset again to generate a new one.

Managing Region Assignments

  1. Find the user in the Users table.
  2. Click Edit to open their profile, or use the region assignment shortcut if available.
  3. Check or uncheck regions to control which queues the user can see and work in.
  4. Click Save. The user's queue access updates immediately.

21. Admin Panel — Managing Regions Admin Only

Click Admin in the sidebar, then the Regions tab. Regions define how tickets are organized and how emails are routed.

Viewing Regions

The Regions tab shows all configured regions with their name, routing aliases, and status (active/inactive).

Creating a New Region

  1. Click Create Region (or the + button).
  2. Enter the Region Name (e.g., "Central PA", "South NJ", "Delaware Valley").
  3. Enter Routing Aliases — these are the email addresses that, when an email is sent to them, will cause the ticket to be routed to this region. Enter multiple aliases separated by commas.
    Example: centralpa@seniorityhealthcare.com, region1@seniorityhealthcare.com
  4. Click Save. The region is created and immediately available for ticket routing and user assignment.

How Email Routing Works

When an inbound email arrives and the system detects that it was sent to (or CC'd to) an address matching a region's routing alias, the resulting ticket is automatically placed in that region's queue. This enables automatic routing without manual intervention.

Tip: Configure routing aliases to match the actual email addresses used in your Google Workspace. For example, if your team uses centralpa@seniorityhealthcare.com for the Central PA region, add that as a routing alias for the Central PA region.

Editing a Region

  1. Find the region in the list and click Edit.
  2. Modify the name or routing aliases as needed.
  3. Click Save. Changes take effect immediately. Existing tickets in this region are not affected.

Deactivating a Region

  1. Click Deactivate next to the region.
  2. A confirmation prompt warns: "Deactivate [Region]? Only works if no open tickets remain."
  3. If there are open tickets in the region, the deactivation will fail. You must close or move all tickets first.
  4. Once deactivated, the region no longer accepts new tickets via email routing and does not appear in region dropdowns for new tickets.
Warning: Before deactivating a region, ensure all open tickets have been closed or moved to another region. Deactivation is blocked if open tickets exist.

22. Admin Panel — Managing Tags Supervisor+

Click Admin in the sidebar, then the Tags tab. Tags help categorize and filter tickets. Both Supervisors and Admins can manage tags.

Creating a Tag

  1. At the top of the Tags tab, you see a color palette with 24 preset colors. Click a color to select it for your new tag.
  2. The selected color appears as a preview circle next to the text input.
  3. Type the tag name in the text field (e.g., "Authorization", "Follow-up", "Urgent").
  4. Click + Add Tag or press Enter.
  5. The new tag appears in the list and is immediately available for use on tickets.

Editing a Tag

  1. Find the tag in the list and click Edit.
  2. The row switches to edit mode: you can change the color (click a new color from the palette) and the name (edit the text field).
  3. Click Save to apply changes, or Cancel to discard.
  4. Changes are reflected immediately on all tickets that already have this tag.

Deleting a Tag

  1. Find the tag and click Delete.
  2. A confirmation prompt warns: "Delete tag [name]? It will be removed from all tickets."
  3. Click OK. The tag is permanently removed from the system and stripped from all tickets.
Warning: Deleting a tag removes it from every ticket in the system, including archived tickets. This cannot be undone. Consider renaming a tag instead if you just want to change its label.

Tag Information

Each tag in the list shows:

  • The colored dot representing the tag's color
  • The tag name
  • The tag's internal ID (in monospace font, useful for technical reference)
  • Edit and Delete buttons

23. Google Workspace Integration Admin Only

Seniority Connect integrates with Google Workspace using a service account delegation model. This powers email sync, contacts autocomplete, calendar access, and Google Drive integration.

How It Works

The integration uses a Google Workspace service account with domain-wide delegation. This means:

  • The service account can access Gmail, Calendar, and Drive on behalf of any user in the domain
  • Each user's Workspace connection must be authorized individually (see Section 24)
  • Email sync runs automatically for connected users, pulling new emails into the ticket queue

What Workspace Powers

FeatureDescription
Email SyncInbound emails are automatically detected and created as tickets in the appropriate region queue
Send EmailsOutbound replies and new messages are sent through the user's Gmail account
ContactsAutocomplete suggestions pulled from Gmail sent/received history
Personal InboxFull Gmail inbox access within Seniority Connect (read, reply, archive, delete, label, star)
CalendarAccess to Google Calendar (available via sidebar links)
DriveAccess to Google Drive (available via sidebar links)

External Application Links

The sidebar includes direct links to external Google Workspace tools (Calendar, Drive, Docs, etc.) as well as other external systems (Practice Fusion, Updox, CareLink, Prompted). Each opens in a new browser tab.

Tip: If email sync stops working for a user, check their Workspace connection status in the Admin Panel > Users tab. The connection may need to be re-established.

24. Connecting Workspace for Other Users Admin Only

As an Admin, you can connect and disconnect Google Workspace for any user in the system. This is essential for new users and for troubleshooting email sync issues.

Checking Connection Status

  1. Go to Admin > Users tab.
  2. For each user, you will see their Workspace status:
    • Connected — Workspace is linked. The connected email address is shown.
    • Not connected — Workspace needs to be set up.

Connecting Workspace for a User

  1. Find the user in the Admin > Users table.
  2. Click the Connect Workspace button next to their name.
  3. A Google OAuth authorization window opens in a popup (width 500px, height 600px).
  4. In the popup, sign in with the user's Google Workspace account (their @seniorityhealthcare.com Gmail account) and grant the requested permissions.
  5. Once authorized, the popup closes automatically.
  6. The user's status in the table updates to "Connected" with their email address shown.
  7. A toast notification confirms: "Workspace connected for [email]."
Important: You need access to the user's Google account credentials or the user's cooperation to complete the OAuth flow. The popup requires signing into their specific Google account.

Disconnecting Workspace for a User

  1. Find the connected user in the Admin > Users table.
  2. Click Disconnect Workspace.
  3. The connection is removed immediately. The user's status changes to "Not connected."
  4. A toast notification confirms: "Workspace disconnected."

When Workspace is disconnected for a user:

  • Email sync stops — new emails will not be pulled into the ticket queue for this user
  • They can no longer send replies or new messages from within Seniority Connect
  • Their personal email inbox will not load
  • Contacts autocomplete will not function
  • Existing tickets and data are not affected — only the live email connection is severed
Tip: If a user reports that their email is not syncing or they cannot send replies, the first thing to check is their Workspace connection status. Disconnecting and reconnecting often resolves token-related issues.

Admin Tips & Best Practices

  • Onboard new users completely: After sending an invitation, remember to connect their Workspace once they have completed account setup. Without Workspace, they cannot use email features.
  • Audit region aliases regularly: Make sure each region's routing aliases match the actual email addresses in use. Mismatched aliases cause emails to land in the wrong region or not be routed at all.
  • Use the Invitations tab proactively: Check for expired invitations periodically and resend as needed.
  • Tag management affects everyone: Deleting a tag removes it system-wide. Rename instead of delete when possible.
  • Deactivate, don't delete: When a user leaves, deactivate their account. This preserves their ticket history and audit trail while preventing login.
  • Monitor Workspace connections: Periodically check the Users table for disconnected Workspace accounts, especially after Google password changes or security events.
  • Use the User Audit to evaluate team performance before making staffing or assignment changes.
  • The Dashboard's Bird's Eye View is your best tool for real-time situational awareness across all regions.
