# Seniority Connect -- Screen Recording Video Scripts

> **Purpose:** Step-by-step scripts for recording training videos with Loom (or similar).
> Each script tells you exactly what to show on screen, what to say, and where to add annotations.

---

## 1. Welcome to Seniority Connect

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | ~2 minutes |
| **Audience** | All new users (coordinators, supervisors, admins) |
| **Goal** | Give viewers a mental map of the app before they do anything |

### Script

#### INTRO (0:00 -- 0:20)

**Show on screen:** Browser open to the Seniority Connect login page. Log in so the main workspace loads.

**Say:**
"Hey there -- welcome to Seniority Connect. This is the platform your team uses every day to manage care coordination tickets, communicate with providers, and keep everything organized. In this quick video I'll walk you through the interface so you know where everything lives before you start working."

---

#### THE SIDEBAR (0:20 -- 0:50)

**Show on screen:** Move your cursor slowly down the left sidebar, hovering over each icon.

**Say:**
"On the left you've got the sidebar. This is your main navigation. At the top you'll see your Region Queue -- that's where incoming tickets land. Below that is your personal Email inbox, then your Chat panel, and the AI assistant. If you're a supervisor or admin you'll also see Dashboard and Admin links down here."

**Annotate:**
- Circle each sidebar icon as you mention it.
- Arrow from the Region Queue icon to the main panel to show the relationship.

---

#### TABS AND PANELS (0:50 -- 1:25)

**Show on screen:** Open a ticket so it appears as a tab. Open a second ticket in another tab. Click between them. Then show the right-side detail panel.

**Say:**
"Seniority Connect uses a tab system, kind of like your browser. Every ticket, email, or chat you open gets its own tab at the top. You can have several open at once and flip between them. On the right side of any ticket you'll see the details panel -- that shows you who's assigned, the status, tags, response time, all that metadata."

**Annotate:**
- Arrow pointing to the tab bar.
- Circle the detail panel on the right.

---

#### GETTING HELP (1:25 -- 1:55)

**Show on screen:** Open the AI panel from the sidebar. Type a quick question like "How do I reassign a ticket?" Show the response.

**Say:**
"If you ever get stuck, you've got a couple of options. You can open the Seniority AI panel right here in the sidebar and ask it a question. It knows about your caseload and can help with tasks. You can also reach out to your supervisor or check the user manual. We'll cover all of these features in detail in the other videos in this series."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the AI panel icon in the sidebar.

---

#### WRAP-UP (1:55 -- 2:00)

**Show on screen:** Return to the Region Queue view.

**Say:**
"That's the lay of the land. Next up we'll cover logging in for the first time and setting up your account."

## 2. First Login & Account Setup

|---|---|
| **Duration** | ~3 minutes |

---

**Show on screen:** Open the invitation email. Highlight the "Set Up Your Account" button/link.

**Say:**
"When your admin adds you to the system, you'll get an email that looks like this. It has your name, your assigned role, and a link to set your password. Go ahead and click that link -- it's only valid for a limited time, so don't wait too long."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the setup button/link.

#### SETTING YOUR PASSWORD (0:45 -- 1:15)

**Say:**
"You'll land on this page where you create your password. Pick something strong -- at least twelve characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Type it again to confirm, then hit Submit. You'll get a confirmation that your password was set."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the password requirements text.

---

#### SETTING UP 2FA (1:15 -- 1:50)

**Say:**

**Say:**
"Now let's log in for real. Enter your email, your new password, and the 2FA code from your authenticator app. Hit Log In and you'll land in your workspace. **Annotate:**
- Circle the 2FA code field.

#### IMPORTING PENDING TICKETS (2:25 -- 2:50)

**Show on screen:** Navigate to the Region Queue. Show any tickets in the Unassigned folder. Demonstrate pulling a ticket to your own inbox.

**Say:**
"If there are already tickets waiting for your region, you'll see them in the Unassigned folder of your Region Queue. You can pull one into your own inbox by selecting it and clicking Assign to Me. We'll cover working the queue in detail in the next video."

---

#### WRAP-UP (2:50 -- 3:00)

## 3. Working the Queue

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | ~4 minutes |
| **Audience** | Coordinators and supervisors |
| **Goal** | Master the Region Queue: layout, search, filter, open, bulk actions |

### Script

#### INTRO (0:00 -- 0:15)

**Show on screen:** Click the Region Queue icon in the sidebar so the queue panel is front and center.

**Say:**
"The Region Queue is where all the action starts. In this video I'll show you how the queue is organized, how to find specific tickets, and how to manage them efficiently."

---

#### QUEUE LAYOUT (0:15 -- 0:55)

**Show on screen:** Expand the Region Queue folders one by one: Unassigned, then each Pending To folder. Hover over a few tickets to show the preview.

**Say:**
"The queue is divided into folders. At the top you've got Unassigned -- these are tickets that came in but haven't been picked up by anyone yet. Below that you'll see Pending To folders. Each one groups tickets that are waiting on a specific action or person. Think of Unassigned as 'needs an owner' and Pending To as 'has an owner but is waiting on something.' Click any folder to see its tickets listed out."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the Unassigned folder label.
- Circle a Pending To folder label.
- Arrow showing the ticket list that appears when a folder is selected.

---

#### SEARCHING (0:55 -- 1:35)

**Show on screen:** Click into the search bar at the top of the queue. Type a patient name or ticket number. Show the flat list of results.

**Say:**
"Need to find a specific ticket? Use the search bar at the top. Type a patient name, a ticket number, or a keyword. Results come back as a flat list -- they're not grouped by folder, so you'll see everything that matches across the entire queue. Click any result to open it."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the search bar.
- Arrow pointing to the flat result list.

---

#### FILTERING (1:35 -- 2:15)

**Show on screen:** Click the filter icon or open the filter panel. Apply a status filter (e.g., "Open"). Then add a region filter. Then add a tag filter. Show the queue updating after each filter.

**Say:**
"For more targeted browsing, use the filters. You can filter by status -- Open, Pending, Closed -- by region, or by tags. Filters stack, so you can say 'show me all Open tickets in the Southeast region tagged Urgent.' The queue updates instantly as you change filters. Clear them when you're done to see everything again."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the filter icon/button.
- Circle each filter dropdown as you use it.

---

#### OPENING TICKETS INTO TABS (2:15 -- 2:45)

**Show on screen:** Click a ticket in the queue. Show it opening as a new tab. Open a second ticket. Show both tabs. Click between them.

**Say:**
"Click any ticket to open it in a new tab. You can open several at once -- each one gets its own tab, just like browser tabs. This is great when you're comparing tickets or working on a few at the same time. Click the X on a tab to close it when you're done."

**Annotate:**
- Arrow from queue list item to the new tab that appears.

---

#### BULK ACTIONS (2:45 -- 3:45)

**Show on screen:** Check the selection checkbox on two or three tickets in the queue. Show the bulk action bar that appears. Demonstrate "Reassign" by picking a team member. Then demonstrate "Pull from Queue" on an unassigned ticket.

**Say:**
"When you need to act on multiple tickets at once, use bulk actions. Check the boxes next to the tickets you want, and this action bar appears at the top. You can Reassign them to another team member -- just pick the person from the dropdown. You can also Pull from Queue, which moves unassigned tickets into your own inbox so you can start working them. This saves a ton of time compared to opening each ticket one by one."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the selection checkboxes.
- Circle the bulk action bar.
- Arrow from the Reassign button to the team member dropdown.

---

#### WRAP-UP (3:45 -- 4:00)

**Show on screen:** Return to the queue view with a clean set of filters.

**Say:**
"That's the queue in a nutshell -- folders, search, filters, tabs, and bulk actions. Next we'll go deep on what happens inside a ticket."

---

## 4. Ticket Deep Dive

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | ~5 minutes |
| **Audience** | All users |
| **Goal** | Thorough walkthrough of everything inside a ticket |

### Script

#### INTRO (0:00 -- 0:15)

**Show on screen:** Open a ticket from the queue so it fills the main panel.

**Say:**
"Let's take a deep dive into a ticket. This is where you'll spend most of your time, so it's worth knowing every piece of it."

---

#### TICKET LAYOUT (0:15 -- 0:45)

**Show on screen:** Slowly pan across the ticket view. Show the message thread in the center, the detail panel on the right, and the action buttons at the top/bottom.

**Say:**
"When you open a ticket, you'll see the message thread front and center -- that's the conversation history. On the right is the details panel with metadata like who's assigned, the status, tags, and timestamps. At the top or bottom you'll find your action buttons -- Reply, Note, Chat, and AI actions."

**Annotate:**
- Label three zones: "Message Thread" (center), "Details" (right), "Actions" (top/bottom).

---

#### READING THE MESSAGE THREAD (0:45 -- 1:15)

**Show on screen:** Scroll through a ticket with multiple messages. Point out timestamps, sender names, and the chronological order.

**Say:**
"The message thread shows every message in chronological order. Each one has the sender's name, the timestamp, and the full text. Scroll down to see the most recent messages. If there are a lot of messages, this is where the AI summarize feature really comes in handy -- but we'll get to that in a moment."

**Annotate:**
- Circle a sender name and timestamp on one message.

---

#### COMPOSE REPLY VS REPLY ALL (1:15 -- 1:50)

**Show on screen:** Click "Reply" to open the reply composer. Type a short message. Then close it and click "Reply All" instead. Point out the recipient list difference.

**Say:**
"To respond, click Reply to send to just the original sender, or Reply All to include everyone on the thread. Check the To field -- Reply All will list every participant. Pick whichever is appropriate. Type your message and hit Send."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the "To" field showing recipient differences between Reply and Reply All.

---

#### INTERNAL NOTES (1:50 -- 2:15)

**Show on screen:** Click the "Internal Note" button. Type a note like "Spoke with patient's daughter, confirmed address." Submit it. Show it appearing in the thread with a visual distinction (different color/label).

**Say:**
"Internal notes are messages that only your team can see -- the external parties on the ticket never see these. Use them to log phone calls, add context, or leave instructions for a colleague. They show up in the thread with a different color so you can tell them apart at a glance."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the visual indicator that marks a note as internal.

---

#### STARTING A CHAT (2:15 -- 2:45)

**Show on screen:** Click the "Start Chat" or "Chat" button on the ticket. Show the dialog to add team members. Add one or two members. Send a quick chat message.

**Say:**
"Need a quick sidebar conversation about this ticket? Click Chat to start a team chat linked to this ticket. Add the team members you want to loop in, type your message, and send. This chat stays connected to the ticket, so anyone in the chat can click through to see the full ticket context."

**Annotate:**
- Arrow from the chat button to the member-add dialog.

---

#### AI ACTIONS (2:45 -- 3:30)

**Show on screen:** Click the AI actions menu on the ticket. Run "Summarize" and show the summary appearing. Run "Patient Info" and show the extracted info. Run "Draft Reply" and show the draft populating the compose area.

**Say:**
"Here's where the AI really shines. On any ticket you can run AI actions. Summarize gives you a quick recap of the entire thread -- super useful for long tickets. Patient Info pulls out key details like name, DOB, and insurance. Draft Reply generates a starting point for your response based on the conversation. You can edit the draft before sending -- it's a first draft, not the final word."

**Annotate:**
- Circle each AI action button as you use it.
- Arrow from the Draft Reply output to the compose area.

---

#### TIME CLOCK (3:30 -- 4:05)

**Show on screen:** Open a ticket and point out the time clock starting automatically. Work in the ticket for a few seconds. Then manually stop the clock. Show the time log.

**Say:**
"Every ticket has a time clock. It auto-starts when you open the ticket so you don't have to remember to track your time. When you're done working, stop it manually or it stops when you navigate away. You can view the time log to see a breakdown of how much time you've spent. This data feeds into the supervisor dashboards."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the time clock indicator.
- Circle the time log section.

---

#### TICKET DETAILS (4:05 -- 4:35)

**Show on screen:** In the right-side details panel, point out each field: Assigned to, Read status, Response Time, Status, Tags.

**Say:**
"The details panel on the right gives you the full picture. You can see who's assigned, whether the ticket has been read, the response time -- that's how long since the last external message -- the status, and any tags. You can edit most of these directly from this panel."

**Annotate:**
- Label each field in the details panel.

---

#### CLOSING A TICKET (4:35 -- 4:55)

**Show on screen:** Click the Close or Resolve button on the ticket. Show the confirmation. Mention that the linked chat gets archived.

**Say:**
"When a ticket is resolved, close it. Hit Close, confirm, and it moves to the archive. Any team chat linked to this ticket also gets archived automatically. If you need to reopen it later, a supervisor can do that from the archive."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the Close button.

---

#### WRAP-UP (4:55 -- 5:00)

**Show on screen:** Return to the queue.

**Say:**
"That's everything inside a ticket. Next up, let's look at email and composing new messages."

---

## 5. Email & Compose

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | ~3 minutes |
| **Audience** | All users |
| **Goal** | Cover the personal email inbox, composing, drafts, and pushing to queue |

### Script

#### INTRO (0:00 -- 0:15)

**Show on screen:** Click the Email icon in the sidebar to open your personal inbox.

**Say:**
"Besides the ticket queue, you also have a personal email inbox in Seniority Connect. Let's walk through how email works."

---

#### PERSONAL EMAIL INBOX (0:15 -- 0:45)

**Show on screen:** Show the inbox with a few emails listed. Click on one to open it. Show the email content.

**Say:**
"Your personal inbox shows emails sent directly to you -- not to the shared region address. These are one-to-one communications. Click any email to read it. You'll see the full message, attachments if there are any, and the sender info."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the Email icon in the sidebar.
- Arrow from inbox list to the opened email.

---

#### READING AND REPLYING (0:45 -- 1:20)

**Show on screen:** With an email open, click Reply. Type a short response. Send it.

**Say:**
"Replying works just like you'd expect. Click Reply, type your message, and send. The reply stays threaded with the original email so you can follow the whole conversation. If you need to loop someone in, you can add them to the CC field."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the Reply button and the CC field.

---

#### COMPOSING NEW MESSAGES (1:20 -- 1:55)

**Show on screen:** Click "Compose" or "New Message." Start typing a contact name in the To field and show the autocomplete dropdown. Select a contact. Type a subject and body. Send.

**Say:**
"To send a new email, click Compose. Start typing a name or email in the To field and you'll get autocomplete suggestions from your contacts. Pick the person, add a subject, write your message, and send. It's fast because you don't have to remember exact email addresses."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the autocomplete dropdown.

---

#### MINIMIZING A DRAFT (1:55 -- 2:20)

**Show on screen:** Start composing an email but don't send it. Click the minimize button. Show the draft appearing as a small bar at the bottom. Click it to restore.

**Say:**
"If you need to pause on a draft -- maybe you need to look something up -- just minimize it. It collapses to a small bar at the bottom of your screen. Click it to bring it back and keep writing. Your draft isn't lost."

**Annotate:**
- Arrow from the minimize button to the collapsed draft bar.

---

#### PUSH TO QUEUE (2:20 -- 2:50)

**Show on screen:** With an email open, click "Push to Queue." Show the email converting into a ticket in the region queue.

**Say:**
"Sometimes a personal email is really a care coordination issue that the whole team needs to see. In that case, use Push to Queue. This converts the email into a ticket in the region queue where it can be assigned and tracked just like any other ticket. The original email thread carries over."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the "Push to Queue" button.
- Arrow showing the email appearing in the region queue.

---

#### WRAP-UP (2:50 -- 3:00)

**Show on screen:** Return to the inbox.

**Say:**
"That's email in Seniority Connect -- inbox, reply, compose, minimize drafts, and push to queue when needed. Next, let's talk about team chat."

**Show on screen:** Show the main dashboard. Point out KPIs at the top (open tickets, average response time, etc.). Scroll to the Bird's Eye View section showing tickets by status or region.

**Say:**
"The dashboard gives you the big picture. At the top are your KPIs -- total open tickets, average response time, tickets closed this week, things like that. Below that is the Bird's Eye View, which breaks everything down by status and region. You can see at a glance where the bottlenecks are."

**Annotate:**
- Circle each KPI card.
- Label the Bird's Eye View section.

---

#### ACTIVITY DASHBOARD (0:55 -- 1:35)

**Show on screen:** Navigate to the Activity Dashboard. Show the "Who's Online" indicator. Show team performance metrics (tickets handled, response times by person).

**Say:**
"The Activity Dashboard shows you what's happening right now. Who's Online tells you which team members are currently logged in and active. Below that you'll see team performance -- how many tickets each person has handled, their average response times, and their current workload. Use this to spot if someone's overwhelmed or if the queue needs rebalancing."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the "Who's Online" section.
- Circle the performance metrics table.

---

#### USER AUDIT (1:35 -- 2:15)

**Show on screen:** Navigate to User Audit. Select a team member. Show their response times, action breakdown (replies sent, notes added, tickets closed, time logged).

**Say:**
"User Audit lets you drill into an individual team member's activity. Pick a person and you'll see their response times over a period, how many replies they've sent, notes added, tickets closed, and time logged. This is great for one-on-one check-ins and performance reviews."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the user selector.
- Circle the action breakdown chart.

---

#### AUDIT LOG (2:15 -- 2:50)

**Show on screen:** Navigate to the Audit Log. Scroll through entries. Click a ticket link in one of the log entries to open the ticket.

**Say:**
"The Audit Log is a chronological record of every action taken in the system -- who did what, when, and on which ticket. Each entry includes a clickable ticket link so you can jump straight to the ticket in question. This is your paper trail."

**Annotate:**
- Circle a clickable ticket link in the audit log.

---

#### PULL FROM QUEUE (2:50 -- 3:25)

**Show on screen:** Go to the Region Queue. Select an unassigned ticket. Click "Pull from Queue." Show the option to keep the ticket in the original queue vs. move it to your personal inbox. Choose "My Inbox" and show the ticket moving.

**Say:**
"As a supervisor, you can Pull from Queue just like coordinators, but you'll see the option to keep the ticket in the original queue or move it into your personal inbox. Keeping it in the original queue is useful when you want to review it but leave it available for coordinators. Moving it to your inbox is for when you're taking ownership."

**Annotate:**
- Circle the "Original Queue" vs "My Inbox" options.

---

#### REOPENING TICKETS FROM ARCHIVE (3:25 -- 3:50)

## Recording Checklist

Before you hit record on each video, run through this checklist:

- [ ] **Clean workspace** -- close unnecessary tabs, clear notifications, use test/demo data
- [ ] **Resolution** -- set your browser to 1920x1080 or 1280x720 for crisp recordings
- [ ] **Zoom level** -- browser zoom at 100% (or 110% if text is small)
- [ ] **Microphone** -- test audio levels; use a headset or external mic if possible
- [ ] **Notifications** -- turn off system and browser notifications (Slack, email, OS)
- [ ] **Script printed or on second screen** -- don't wing it; follow the script
- [ ] **Annotations ready** -- know which tool you'll use for circles/arrows (Loom has built-in drawing)
- [ ] **Dry run** -- do one practice run without recording to check flow and timing
- [ ] **Intro slide** -- optionally record a 3-second title card with the video name and duration
