1. Getting Started

Seniority Connect is the care coordination platform for Seniority Healthcare. It provides a centralized workspace for managing patient and provider communications, team collaboration, email, time tracking, and AI-powered assistance.

What Seniority Connect Does

  • Ticket Management — Track and manage all communications as tickets with statuses, tags, assignments, regions, and time tracking.
  • Smart Routing — All incoming emails land in the unassigned queue. Users import the tickets addressed to them on login, or claim them manually.
  • Email Integration — Full Gmail inbox access with contacts autocomplete, label management, compose, reply, and AI-assisted drafting.
  • Team Chat — Start chats directly from tickets. Members are added explicitly and stay until they leave.
  • Time Tracking — Clock auto-starts when you open a ticket. Per-user time breakdown with expandable session details.
  • AI Assistant — Powered by Claude, the AI can summarize tickets, extract patient data, draft replies, search emails, and answer questions about your caseload using 8 dynamic tools.
  • Dashboards & Analytics — Real-time dashboards, user audit with response time tracking, activity heatmaps, and online presence.
  • Archive — Closed tickets live in a searchable archive with full history preserved.
  • Administration — Manage users, regions, tags, and system settings.

Ticket Tab Bar

When you open tickets, they appear as tabs at the top of the content area. You can:

  • Open multiple tickets simultaneously from the queue.
  • Click a tab to switch between open tickets.
  • Click X on a tab to close it (stops the time clock for that ticket).
  • Click + at the end of the tab bar to return to the queue and open another ticket.
  • Tabs persist when you navigate to other screens — click any tab to jump back.

Right-Side Panels

Chat and Seniority AI open as resizable panels on the right side. You can drag the panel edge to resize, and both can be open simultaneously.

4. Logging In & Account Setup

New User Setup

  1. You will receive an invitation email with a setup link.
  2. Click the link to open the setup wizard.
  3. Create a password (minimum 8 characters).
  4. Set up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) by scanning the QR code with an authenticator app.
  5. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator to confirm.
  6. You're ready to log in!

Logging In

  1. Enter your email address and password.
  2. Enter the 6-digit 2FA code from your authenticator app.
  3. If there are unassigned tickets addressed to you, a Pending Import modal will appear.

Pending Import on Login

When you sign in, if there are unassigned messages in the queue that were addressed to you, a modal will show:

  • A list of all pending tickets with checkboxes, ticket ID, subject, and sender.
  • Select All / Select None links to quickly toggle all.
  • A counter showing "X of Y selected".
  • Import Selected — Claims the checked tickets into your personal queue.
  • Leave All in Queue — Dismisses the modal; tickets stay unassigned for anyone to claim.
Tip: All tickets are pre-selected by default. Uncheck any you don't want before importing.

Work Status (Coordinators)

Coordinators can toggle their status between Active and Inactive using the button at the bottom of the sidebar.

  • Active — You can claim and work tickets normally.
  • Inactive — Your assigned tickets are returned to the region queue. Email sync stops for your account.

5. Ticket Queues & Routing

How Routing Works

All incoming emails land in the unassigned queue. No auto-assignment occurs. Instead:

  • Tickets are tagged with the intended recipient (the coordinator whose inbox received the email).
  • In the queue, unassigned tickets show "Pending To: [Name]" collapsible sub-folders inside the Unassigned group.
  • If an email was sent to multiple coordinators, the ticket appears in each person's folder with a purple "Sent to N" badge.
  • When someone claims a ticket, it moves out of all Pending To folders into their personal queue.
  • Tickets with no specific intended recipient show directly in the Unassigned group.

Region Queue

The Region Queue shows all tickets across your assigned regions, grouped by assignee. Unassigned tickets appear first with "Pending To" sub-folders.

  • Click a group header to expand/collapse.
  • Filter by status: Active, Unassigned, Open, Waiting, Closed.
  • Filter by region: Use the region dropdown.
  • Search: Type in the search box — results show as a flat list (no grouping needed).

Ticket IDs

New tickets get short, meaningful IDs based on the region:

  • CPA-0001 — Central PA, ticket #1
  • SNJ-0042 — South NJ, ticket #42
  • DV-0003 — Delaware Valley, ticket #3

Region Alias Routing

Emails sent to a region alias (e.g., region1@seniorityhealthcare.com) are automatically routed to the correct region based on the alias configured in Admin > Regions.

My Queue

My Queue shows only tickets assigned to you. Same filters and search apply.

Bulk Actions

Use checkboxes to select multiple tickets, then:

  • Pull from Queue — Remove selected tickets and return emails to Gmail (supervisors/admins).
  • Reassign To... — Reassign selected tickets to any team member or return to queue. All roles can use this (coordinators can only assign to themselves).

Reassigned tickets appear as unread/new for the recipient.

6. Working with Tickets

Ticket Detail View

Click any ticket to open it. You'll see:

  • Header — Ticket ID, status badge, subject, and a time clock.
  • Message Thread — All inbound and outbound messages chronologically.
  • Action Tabs — Reply, Internal Note, Start a Chat / Open Chat, Seniority AI.
  • Sidebar — Assignee, region, tags, status controls, details (timestamps, response time), and time log.

Replying to a Ticket

  1. Click the Reply tab.
  2. Type your message. Optionally click AI Draft for AI-generated text.
  3. Attach files if needed (max 10MB per file).
  4. Click Send.
Note: Replying to a ticket from the regional queue automatically assigns it to you.

Internal Notes

  1. Click the Internal Note tab.
  2. Type your note and click Add Note.
Tip: Notes are only visible to your team — never sent to external parties.

Audit Details

The Details panel in the ticket sidebar shows:

FieldDescription
ReceivedWhen the ticket was created.
AssignedWhen it was assigned to someone.
ReadWhen the assignee first opened it, and who read it.
Response TimeTime between assigned and read. Color-coded: green (<1h), yellow (1-4h), red (>4h).

Time LoggedTotal time tracked on this ticket.

Closing a Ticket

Click Close, select a closure reason, and optionally add a comment. When a ticket is closed:

  • Any active chat is archived as a note on the ticket (full transcript preserved).
  • The chat channel is removed from the Chat panel.
  • The ticket moves to the Archive.
  • Internal notes remain on the ticket permanently.

7. Ticket Tabs & Multi-Tasking

You can have multiple tickets open simultaneously:

  • Opening a ticket from the queue adds it as a tab at the top.
  • Click between tabs to switch tickets.
  • Click + to go back to the queue and open another ticket without closing existing tabs.
  • Click X on a tab to close it (this stops the time clock for that ticket).
  • Tabs persist when you navigate to other screens (Dashboard, Email, etc.) — click any tab to return.

8. Time Tracking

The time clock tracks how long each user spends on a ticket.

How It Works

  • The clock auto-starts when you open a ticket and continues from the previous total.
  • A red pulsing button in the ticket header shows the running time.
  • Click Stop to manually pause the clock. It will not auto-restart until you click Start Clock again.
  • Closing a ticket tab stops the clock automatically.
  • Only one clock can run at a time — starting a new one auto-stops the previous.

Time Log

In the ticket sidebar, the Time Spent section shows:

  • Total time across all users.
  • Per-user breakdown showing each person's contribution.
  • Click "Show N entries" to expand and see individual sessions with exact date, start time, and stop time.

9. Creating New Messages

Click the New Message button in the sidebar to open the compose modal.

Compose Fields

  • To (Email) — Start typing to see contact suggestions from Gmail history and existing tickets.
  • Subject — The email subject line (required).
  • Send from Region — Select which region this ticket belongs to (required).
  • Tags — Optionally tag the new ticket.
  • Message Body — Write your message or click AI Draft to have the AI generate one.
  • Attachments — Upload files (max 10MB each).

Minimize & Resume

Click the — (minimize) button next to X to minimize the compose modal. A draft bar appears at the bottom-right showing the subject. Click it to resume editing. Click X on the bar to discard.

Note: Clicking outside the compose modal does not close it — you must use X or Cancel to prevent accidental loss of drafts.

10. Personal Email Inbox

The Email screen provides full access to your Gmail inbox directly within Seniority Connect.

Navigation

  • Left sidebar — System folders (Inbox, Starred, Sent, Drafts, etc.), Categories, and your Gmail Labels.
  • Search bar — Search across all mail.

Reading & Managing Email

Click any message to open the full view. From the detail view you can Reply, AI Draft, Archive, Delete, Apply Labels, Star, or Mark Unread.

Push to Queue

Supervisors Admins can push individual or bulk-selected emails to the ticket queue using the Push to Queue button.

11. Contacts & Autocomplete

When composing a new message or email, the To field provides contact autocomplete:

  • Contacts are pulled from your Gmail sent/received history (last 200 messages each direction).
  • Also includes all external participants from existing tickets.
  • Search by name, email, or organization.
  • Contacts are cached for 10 minutes for fast lookup.

12. Gmail Labels

All your Gmail labels are synced into Seniority Connect, including nested labels and colors.

  • View by label — Click any label in the email sidebar.
  • Apply labels — Open a message, click the label icon, check/uncheck labels.
  • Create labels — Click + next to "Labels" in the email sidebar.
  • Rename/Delete — Right-click any label for options.

13. Team Chat

Starting a Chat from a Ticket

In the ticket detail view, the chat tab shows:

  • "Start a Chat" (gray) — No chat exists yet. Click to create one.
  • "Open Chat" (blue) — A chat already exists. Click to view and continue the conversation.

Chat Membership

  • Only the creator is added initially.
  • Click "+ Add Team Members" to invite others.
  • Members stay in the chat until they choose to leave (remove from their chat list).
  • Anyone who opens the ticket and clicks the chat tab is auto-joined.

Chat from the Chat Panel

The sidebar Chat panel shows all your conversations. For ticket chats:

  • Ticket chats show as "Chat from [sender name]" for recipients.
  • Click "View Ticket" to open the ticket with the chat visible.
  • Ticket icon in the channel list is also clickable.

Chat Archival

When a ticket is closed, the chat transcript is saved as an archived note on the ticket and the chat channel is removed from the Chat panel.

Direct & Group Messages

Click + New in the Chat panel to start direct messages or group conversations outside of tickets.

15. Supervisor Dashboard

Supervisors Admins

Shows KPIs (Total Open, Unassigned, Closed Today, Triage), region breakdown, coordinator workload, and a Bird's Eye View with aging cards and real-time team status.

16. Activity Dashboard & User Audit

Supervisors Admins

Five tabs: Who's Online, Overview, Team, Activity Feed, and User Audit.

User Audit Tab

Select any user and a date range, then click Run Audit to see:

  • Summary cards — Open tickets, unread count, closed count, total actions, average response time (color-coded).
  • Response time details — Per-ticket assigned-to-read times with fastest/slowest/average.
  • Open tickets — Which are read vs unread, with wait times. Click any to open.
  • Action breakdown — Visual counts by action type.
  • Recent actions timeline — Full audit trail with clickable ticket links.

17. Audit Log

Supervisors Admins

Detailed record of every action. All ticket IDs in the log are clickable — click to open the ticket directly. Filter by action type using the dropdown.

18. Archive

The Archive screen shows all closed tickets, sorted by close date. Available to all users.

  • Search across archived tickets by subject, sender, or ticket ID.
  • Pagination — 25 tickets per page.
  • Each ticket shows subject, sender, tags, who handled it, region, and close date.
  • Click any ticket to view full history (messages, notes, archived chat transcript).
  • Reopen (supervisors/admins) — Click the Reopen button to move a ticket back to the active queue.

19. Admin Panel

Users Tab Admin

  • View, edit, deactivate, reactivate users.
  • Manage region assignments and reset passwords.
  • Connect/disconnect Google Workspace per user.

Invitations Tab Admin

  • Send invitations with name, email (@seniorityhealthcare.com), role, and regions.
  • View pending/accepted/expired invitations. Resend or revoke.

Regions Tab Admin

  • Create, edit, deactivate regions.
  • Set routing email aliases (e.g., region1@seniorityhealthcare.com) for automatic email routing.

Tags Tab Supervisors Admins

  • Create, edit, delete tags with custom colors.

20. Google Workspace Integration

Seniority Connect integrates with Google Workspace via service account delegation. Email sync, contacts, calendar, and drive access are all available.

21. Pull from Queue

Supervisors Admins

Click Pull from Queue on a ticket or select multiple tickets and click Pull. A modal asks where the email should go:

  • Return to original recipient — Restores the email to the coordinator who originally received it. Appears as unread.
  • Pull to my inbox — Forwards the email to your own Gmail inbox. Appears as unread.

Both options remove the ticket from Seniority Connect entirely.

22. External Application Links

The sidebar includes direct links to external systems: Practice Fusion, Updox, CareLink, Prompted. Each opens in a new tab.

23. Tips & Best Practices

Working Efficiently

  • Use the AI assistant to draft replies and summarize long threads — it saves significant time.
  • Open multiple tickets in tabs to compare or work on several at once.
  • Tag tickets consistently so the tag filter is useful for your team.
  • The time clock runs automatically — your time is always tracked.
  • Use the minimize feature on compose to save a draft while working on other things.
  • Start a chat on a ticket to discuss it with your team without emailing externally.

For Supervisors

  • Use the User Audit tab to monitor response times and individual performance.
  • Check "Pending To" folders in the Unassigned section to see who has unclaimed messages.
  • The Audit Log has clickable ticket links — click any to jump directly to the ticket.
  • Use Bulk Reassign when a coordinator is overwhelmed or out.
  • The Archive preserves full history including chat transcripts.

Getting Help

Use the Seniority AI assistant — it can answer questions about tickets, patients, team workload, and more. For technical issues, contact your system administrator.
