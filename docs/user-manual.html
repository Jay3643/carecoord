1. Getting Started

CareCoord is a care coordination platform built for Seniority Healthcare. It provides a centralized workspace for managing patient communications, team collaboration, email, and AI-powered assistance.

What CareCoord Does

  • Ticket Management — Track and manage all patient and provider communications as tickets with statuses, tags, assignments, and regions.
  • Email Integration — Full Gmail inbox access with label management, compose, reply, and AI-assisted drafting.
  • Team Chat — Real-time internal messaging between team members.
  • AI Assistant — Powered by Claude, the AI can summarize tickets, extract patient data, draft replies, and answer questions about your caseload.
  • Dashboards & Analytics — Supervisors get real-time dashboards showing team workload, ticket aging, activity tracking, and online presence.
  • Administration — Manage users, regions, tags, and system settings.

4. Logging In & Account Setup

New User Setup

  1. You will receive an invitation email with a setup link.
  2. Click the link to open the setup wizard.
  3. Create a password (minimum 8 characters).
  4. Set up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) by scanning the QR code with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, etc.).
  5. Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator to confirm.
  6. You're ready to log in!

Logging In

  1. Enter your email address and password.
  2. Enter the 6-digit 2FA code from your authenticator app.
  3. You will be taken to the Region Queue.
Note: If you are logging in for the first time with a temporary password, you will be prompted to change your password before proceeding.

Work Status (Coordinators)

Coordinators can toggle their status between Active and Inactive using the button at the bottom of the sidebar.

  • Active — You can receive new ticket assignments.
  • Inactive — Your assigned tickets are returned to the region queue. You will not receive new assignments.

5. Ticket Queues

Region Queue

The Region Queue shows all tickets across your assigned regions, grouped by assignee. Unassigned tickets appear first.

  • Click a group header to expand/collapse that assignee's tickets.
  • Filter by status: Active, Unassigned, Open, Waiting, Closed.
  • Filter by region: Use the region dropdown (if you belong to multiple regions).
  • Search: Type in the search box to find tickets by subject, email, or ticket ID.

My Queue

My Queue shows only tickets assigned to you. It uses a flat list (no grouping). The same filters and search apply.

Sorting by Tags

When tickets have tags, a tag dropdown appears next to the status filters. Select a tag and choose a mode:

  • Filter — Show only tickets with the selected tag.
  • Sort — All tickets visible, but tagged ones appear first.
  • Group (Region Queue only) — Groups tickets by tag instead of assignee.

Bulk Actions

Use the checkboxes to select multiple tickets, then use the Pull from Queue button to assign selected tickets to yourself.

6. Working with Tickets

Ticket Detail View

Click any ticket in a queue to open the full detail view. You'll see:

  • Header — Ticket ID, status badge, subject, from/to emails, assignee, region.
  • Message Thread — All inbound and outbound messages in chronological order.
  • Notes — Internal team notes (not visible to external parties).
  • Attachments — Download files attached to messages.

Replying to a Ticket

  1. Click the Reply tab in the ticket detail.
  2. Type your message in the text area.
  3. Optionally click AI Draft to have the AI write a reply for you.
  4. Attach files if needed (max 10MB per file).
  5. Click Send.

Adding Internal Notes

  1. Click the Notes tab.
  2. Type your note in the text area.
  3. Click Add Note.
Tip: Notes are only visible to your team — they are never sent to external parties.

Managing Ticket Properties

7. Creating New Messages

Click the New Message button in the left sidebar to open the compose modal.

Compose Fields

  • To (Email) — The recipient's email address (required).
  • Subject — The email subject line (required).
  • Send from Region — Select which region this ticket belongs to (required).
  • Tags — Optionally tag the new ticket.
  • Message Body — Write your message or click AI Draft to have the AI generate one.
  • Attachments — Upload files (max 10MB each).

Sending creates a new ticket assigned to you with the outbound message as the first entry.

8. Personal Email Inbox

The Email screen provides full access to your Gmail inbox directly within CareCoord.

Navigation

  • Left sidebar — System folders (Inbox, Starred, Sent, Drafts, etc.), Categories (Primary, Social, Promotions, Updates), and your Gmail Labels.
  • Search bar — Search across all mail.
  • Category tabs — Quick switch between Primary, Social, Promotions, and Updates.

Reading Email

Click any message to open the full view with sender details, body, and attachments. From the detail view you can:

  • Reply — Click Reply to compose a response.
  • AI Draft — Click the teal AI Draft button to have the AI write a reply.
  • Archive / Delete — Use the toolbar icons.
  • Apply Labels — Click the label icon to toggle labels on/off with checkboxes.
  • Star / Mark Unread — Use the toolbar icons.

Composing Email

Supervisors Admins can click the Compose button in the email sidebar to write a new email. Fill in the recipient, subject, body, and optionally attach files or use AI Draft.

Note: Coordinators do not have the Compose button in the email inbox. Instead, use the New Message button in the main sidebar to create ticket-based communications.

Push to Queue

Supervisors Admins can push individual emails or bulk-selected emails to the CareCoord ticket queue using the Push to Queue button.

9. Gmail Labels

All of your Gmail labels are synced into CareCoord, including nested labels and label colors.

Viewing by Label

Click any label in the email sidebar to view all messages with that label.

Applying Labels to Messages

  1. Open a message or select messages with checkboxes.
  2. Click the label/folder icon in the toolbar.
  3. A dropdown appears with all your labels and checkboxes.
  4. Check a label to apply it; uncheck to remove it.

Creating Labels

  1. In the email sidebar, click the + icon next to "Labels".
  2. Type the label name and press Enter or click Create.

Renaming & Deleting Labels

Right-click any label in the sidebar to see a context menu with Rename and Delete options.

Note: Deleting a label does not delete the messages — it only removes the label.

Nested Labels

Labels containing a / (e.g., Work/Urgent) appear indented under their parent. This mirrors Gmail's label hierarchy.

10. Team Chat

Click Chat in the sidebar to open the real-time messaging panel.

Starting a Conversation

  1. Click the + button at the top of the chat panel.
  2. Search for and select team members.
  3. Optionally name the conversation (for groups).
  4. Click Start.

Sending Messages

  • Type your message and press Enter to send.
  • Attach files using the paperclip icon.
  • Messages appear in real-time for all participants.

Notifications

Unread message count appears as a red badge on the Chat sidebar item. The badge pulses to draw attention.

11. Seniority AI Assistant

Click Seniority AI in the sidebar to open the AI panel. The AI has access to all tickets, team data, regions, audit logs, and your recent emails.

General Chat

Ask the AI anything about your caseload:

  • "How many unassigned tickets are in Northern PA?"
  • "Summarize the recent activity for patient John Smith"
  • "What should I work on next?"

Suggestions

When you open the AI panel without a ticket selected, it shows proactive suggestions based on your role and current system state. Click any suggestion to ask the AI about it.

Ticket-Specific Actions

When you have a ticket open, the AI panel shows quick action buttons:

ActionWhat It Does
SummarizeCreates a concise summary of the ticket thread.
Patient InfoExtracts structured patient data (name, DOB, insurance, medications, etc.).
Draft ReplyGenerates a professional reply email.
Suggest TagsRecommends which tags to apply based on ticket content.

AI Draft in Email

When replying to an email or composing a new one, click the teal AI Draft button to have the AI generate the email body for you. You can edit the draft before sending.

12. Supervisor Dashboard

Supervisors Admins

Overview Tab

Shows four KPI cards at the top:

  • Total Open — All non-closed tickets in your regions.
  • Unassigned — Tickets without an assignee.
  • Closed Today — Tickets closed since midnight.
  • Triage Queue — Tickets in the triage/unrouted region.

Below the KPIs you'll find Open Tickets by Region and Coordinator Workload sections. Click any region or coordinator to drill down into their specific tickets.

Bird's Eye View Tab

A real-time operational view showing:

  • Aging cards — Tickets grouped by age: Fresh (<1hr), 1-4 hours, 4-24 hours, 24+ hours. Color-coded green to red.
  • Team status — Each coordinator with online indicator, open ticket count.
  • Region bars — Visual progress bars showing ticket distribution per region.
  • Full ticket table — Filterable by aging bucket, coordinator, region, or status. Includes a Reassign button per ticket.
  • Bulk Reassign — Reassign all of one coordinator's tickets to another (does not move closed tickets).

13. Activity Dashboard

Supervisors Admins

The Activity Dashboard provides detailed analytics and real-time presence tracking. It has four tabs:

Who's Online Tab

Shows all team members with real-time presence status:

  • Online — Active within the last 5 minutes.
  • Away — Has a session but inactive for 5-30 minutes.
  • Offline — No active session or inactive for 30+ minutes.

Drill-Down: Click any team member to see their recent activity. Use the time range selector to view:

  • 15 minutes / 30 minutes / 1 hour / 4 hours / 1 day / 1 week

The drill-down shows:

  • Summary cards: total actions, emails sent, notes added, tickets touched.
  • Action breakdown by type (with colored badges).
  • List of tickets the user worked on.
  • Full chronological activity timeline.

Overview Tab

  • KPI Cards — Tickets Closed, Currently Open, Emails Sent, Total Actions, Avg Resolution Time.
  • Ticket Volume Chart — Daily bar chart of tickets created vs. closed.
  • Activity Heatmap — Day-of-week by hour grid showing when the team is busiest.
  • Tag Distribution — Bar chart showing which tags are most used.

Team Tab

Performance table for all coordinators showing: open tickets, closed tickets, emails sent, notes added, total actions, average resolution time, and a visual activity bar.

Activity Feed Tab

A filterable event stream showing all system actions. Filter by:

  • Specific user
  • Action type (e.g., Created ticket, Sent email, Reassigned, etc.)
  • Time period (24 hours to 30 days)

14. Audit Log

Supervisors Admins

The Audit Log shows a detailed record of every action taken in the system. Each entry includes:

  • Date and time
  • Who performed the action
  • Action type (e.g., ticket_created, outbound_sent, assignee_changed)
  • Details and affected entity

Use the action type dropdown to filter for specific types of activity.

15. Admin Panel

Users Tab Admin

  • View all users with their role, email, assigned regions, and workspace connection status.
  • Edit user — Change name, email, or role.
  • Manage regions — Assign or remove region access per user.
  • Reset password — Generates a temporary password shown in a modal.
  • Deactivate user — Disables the account and returns their tickets to the queue.
  • Reactivate user — Re-enables a deactivated account.
  • Connect Workspace — Manage Google Workspace connection for the user.

Invitations Tab Admin

  • Send invitations to new users with name, email (@seniorityhealthcare.com), role, and region assignments.
  • View pending, accepted, and expired invitations.
  • Resend or revoke pending invitations.

Regions Tab Admin

  • Create, edit, and deactivate regions.
  • Set routing email aliases for each region.
  • View which users are assigned to each region.
Note: A region can only be deactivated if it has no open tickets.

Tags Tab Supervisors Admins

  • Create new tags with a name and color (10 color options).
  • Edit existing tag names and colors.
  • Delete tags.

16. Google Workspace Integration

CareCoord integrates with Google Workspace for email access. The connection is managed per user.

Connecting Your Account

  1. Click the Google Workspace connect button in the sidebar (or have an admin connect it for you).
  2. Choose a sync start date (emails before this date will not be synced).
  3. Complete the Google OAuth authorization in the popup window.
  4. Your Gmail inbox will become available in the Email section.

Sidebar Quick Links

Expand the Google Workspace section in the sidebar to access:

  • Google Calendar, Drive, Docs, Sheets, Meet, Chat, and Voice.

18. Tips & Best Practices

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Enter — Send message in Chat or AI panel.
  • Escape — Close modals, clear search, cancel label creation.

Working Efficiently

  • Use the AI assistant to draft replies and summarize long ticket threads — it saves significant time.
  • Tag your tickets consistently so the tag filter in the queue is useful for your team.
  • Check the Activity Dashboard's "Who's Online" tab to see which team members are available before reassigning tickets.
  • Use the Bird's Eye View to quickly identify aging tickets that need attention.
  • Add internal notes to tickets to keep your team informed without emailing external parties.

For Supervisors

  • Review the Activity Dashboard daily to monitor team performance and identify bottlenecks.
  • Use the drill-down feature on the Who's Online tab to see what each coordinator is working on in real time.
  • The Audit Log is your source of truth for tracking who did what and when.
  • Bulk Reassign is useful when a coordinator is out — it moves all their open tickets to another person or back to the queue (closed tickets are not affected).

Getting Help

If you have questions or need assistance, use the Seniority AI assistant — it can answer questions about tickets, patients, team workload, and more. For technical issues, contact your system administrator.
